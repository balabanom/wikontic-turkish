**Core Components of a Triplet:**

*   **Subject**: The primary entity or concept from which a relation originates. This can be a person, place, organization, event, or abstract idea.
*   **Relation**: A predicate that describes the connection between the subject and the object. These should be concise and descriptive, akin to Wikidata properties.
*   **Object**: The entity or concept that is related to the subject via the specified relation.

**Qualifiers:**

*   Qualifiers provide additional context to a main triplet. They are themselves structured as a relation-object pair, but their "relation" describes how they modify the *main triplet*, not the subject.
*   Qualifiers **must always be attached to a specific triplet** and cannot exist independently.
*   The structure for qualifiers is a list of dictionaries, where each dictionary contains:
    *   `"relation"`: The qualifier's relation.
    *   `"object"`: The qualifier's object.
